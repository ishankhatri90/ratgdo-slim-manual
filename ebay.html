What It Does

Connects your garage opener to your home network for local smart control, real-time status, and automation — no cloud, no monthly fees.

Compatibility

  • Security+ 2.0 (round yellow learn button) — fully supported.
    • Excluded jackshaft models (not supported):
      • 8500 / RJ020
      • 8500C / RJ020C
  • Security+ 1.0 (purple, brown, orange, or red learn button) — experimental support.
    • May not work with all openers or wall control panels.
    • Experimental jackshaft models:
      • 8500 / RJ020
      • 8500C / RJ020C
  • Chamberlain and LiftMaster openers.
  • ESP8266 with 1 MB, 2 MB, or 4 MB flash supported.
  • Dry contact supported in ecosystem — not provided on this board in the current build.

What Makes Slim Special

  • 3-terminal push spring connector — no screwdriver needed.
  • ESP8266 D1 Mini with USB-C port.
  • Socketed ESP module — swap without soldering.
  • SMA connector pin on board — upgrade antenna any time, no tools.
  • Custom 3D-printed vented case for clean mounting.
  • All boards tested before shipping.

⚠ Wire before powering. Incorrect wiring can damage the board or opener.

RED → Wall control RED
WHITE → Ground (GND)
BLACK → Obstruction / control line

Firmware & Setup

Ships pre-flashed and ready to use. Compatible with ESPHome, HomeKit firmware, or Paul Wieland's ratgdo firmware — your choice.

ESPHome ★ Recommended

  • Best for: Apple and Android users, mixed households.
  • Requires: Home Assistant.
  • HomeKit: Via Home Assistant HomeKit bridge.
  • Diagnostics: ESPHome logs + web interface.
  • OTA Updates: Supported.
Steps:
  1. Power opener OFF and complete wiring first.
  2. Power on via USB-C. Connect to ratgdo Wi-Fi AP.
  3. Open 192.168.4.1 → select your 2.4 GHz network.
  4. Add in Home Assistant → Settings → Devices & Services.
  5. Create HomeKit bridge in HA → add to Apple Home.
  6. Test door, light, obstruction sensor. Assign static IP.

HomeKit Direct

  • Best for: Apple-only households, simpler setup.
  • Requires: Apple Home app + HomeKit hub for remote.
  • HomeKit: Native accessory, direct pairing.
  • Diagnostics: Basic device web interface.
  • OTA Updates: Limited.
Steps:
  1. Power opener OFF and complete wiring first.
  2. Power on via USB-C. Connect to Garage-Door-XXXXX (last 5 MAC chars).
  3. Open 192.168.4.1 → select your 2.4 GHz network.
  4. Navigate to device IP → get your HomeKit pairing code.
  5. Add accessory in Apple Home.
  6. Test door, light, sensor. Assign static IP.

Troubleshooting

ESPHome

  • Confirm ratgdo and Home Assistant are on the same 2.4 GHz network.
  • Check ESPHome logs and HA device status for errors.
  • For HomeKit pairing issues, remove and re-add the HomeKit bridge.
  • If door status is wrong, recalibrate open/close travel.
  • Power cycle the device for 10 seconds.

HomeKit Direct

  • Confirm ratgdo and your iPhone/iPad are on the same 2.4 GHz network.
  • Verify your HomeKit hub is online for remote control to work.
  • If pairing fails, reset and re-add the accessory in Apple Home.
  • If needed, factory reset (hold reset 10 s) and reflash.
  • Power cycle the device for 10 seconds.
