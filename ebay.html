ratgdo Slim Garage Door Control Board

Integrate your Chamberlain or LiftMaster opener with Home Assistant and HomeKit, including Siri support.

Advanced users: Home Assistant knowledge required

What It Does

Connects your garage opener to your home network for local smart control, real-time status, and automation.

Compatibility

  • Security+ 2.0 (round yellow learn button): fully supported.
    • Exclusions — jackshaft models not supported:
      • 8500 / RJ020
      • 8500C / RJ020C
  • Security+ 1.0 (purple, brown, orange, red learn button): experimental support.
    • May not work with all openers or wall control panels.
    • Experimental jackshaft models:
      • 8500 / RJ020
      • 8500C / RJ020C
  • ESP8266 with 1MB, 2MB, or 4MB flash supported.
  • Chamberlain and LiftMaster openers (Security+ 1.0 and 2.0 wall-control wiring).
  • Dry contact is supported in the ecosystem but is not provided on this board in the current build.

What Makes Slim Special

  • 3-terminal push spring connector (no screwdriver needed).
  • ESP8266 D1 Mini with USB-C.
  • Socketed ESP module for easy replacement.
  • Custom 3D-printed vented case for clean mounting.
Wire before powering. Incorrect wiring can damage the board.

Firmware & Setup

Ships pre-flashed. Compatible with ESPHome, HomeKit firmware, or Paul Wieland's ratgdo firmware — your choice.

ESPHome (Recommended)

  • Best for: Apple and Android users, mixed-device households.
  • Setup: Moderate — requires Home Assistant.
  • HomeKit: Via Home Assistant HomeKit bridge.
  • Diagnostics: Advanced (ESPHome logs and web interface).
  • OTA Updates: Supported.
Steps:
  1. Power opener OFF and complete wiring.
  2. Power on via USB-C. Connect to ratgdo Wi-Fi AP.
  3. Open 192.168.4.1 and configure your 2.4 GHz network.
  4. Add device in Home Assistant → Settings → Devices & Services.
  5. Create HomeKit bridge in Home Assistant and add to Apple Home.
  6. Test door, light, and obstruction sensor. Assign a static IP.

HomeKit Direct

  • Best for: Apple-only households, simpler setup.
  • Setup: Simple — no Home Assistant required.
  • HomeKit: Native accessory, direct pairing.
  • Diagnostics: Basic device web interface.
  • OTA Updates: Limited availability.
Steps:
  1. Power opener OFF and complete wiring.
  2. Power on via USB-C. Connect to Garage-Door-ABCDE (last 5 MAC chars).
  3. Open 192.168.4.1 and configure your 2.4 GHz network.
  4. Open the device IP to get your HomeKit pairing code.
  5. Add accessory in the Apple Home app.
  6. Test door, light, and sensor. Assign a static IP.

Troubleshooting

ESPHome

  • Confirm ratgdo and Home Assistant are on the same 2.4 GHz network.
  • Check ESPHome logs and HA device status for errors.
  • For HomeKit pairing issues, remove and re-add the HomeKit bridge.
  • If door status is wrong, recalibrate open/close travel.
  • Power cycle the device for 10 seconds.

HomeKit Direct

  • Confirm ratgdo and your iPhone/iPad are on the same 2.4 GHz network.
  • Verify your HomeKit hub is online for remote control to work.
  • If pairing fails, reset and re-add the accessory in Apple Home.
  • If needed, factory reset (hold reset 10 s) and reflash.
  • Power cycle the device for 10 seconds.
